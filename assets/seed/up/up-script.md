## FADE IN:

### 1. A 1930's NEWSREEL.

**NEWSREEL ANNOUNCER (V.O.)**
*"Movietown News" presents — Spotlight on Adventure!*

The mysterious SOUTH AMERICAN JUNGLE. A massive waterfall cascades down a gigantic, flat-topped mountain.

**NEWSREEL ANNOUNCER (V.O.)**
*What you are now witnessing is footage never before seen by civilized humanity: a lost world in South America! Lurking in the shadow of majestic Paradise Falls, it sports plants and animals undiscovered by science. Who would dare set foot on this inhospitable summit?*

A painted portrait of a dashing young adventurer.

**NEWSREEL ANNOUNCER (V.O.)**
*Why, our subject today: Charles Muntz!*

### 1. INT. MOVIE THEATRE — CONTINUOUS

Of everyone watching in the modest, small town theater, no one is more enthralled than 8 year old **CARL FREDRICKSEN**. He stares, mouth agape, wearing leather flight helmet and goggles, just like his idol on the silver screen.

Newsreel footage: the dashing **MUNTZ** descends down the gangplank to the delight of the crowd. His dogs trail him.

**MUNTZ**
*"Adventure is out there!"*

In the theater, Young Carl returns the thumbs up.

Scientists accuse Muntz of fabricating the skeleton of a giant bird. His Explorer's Society badge is ceremoniously RIPPED from his jacket. Carl GASPS.

Muntz grimly addresses a crowd at an airfield:

**MUNTZ**
*I promise to capture the beast — alive! And I will not come back until I do!*

Carl looks like he just witnessed a miracle.

### 2. EXT. SMALL TOWN NEIGHBORHOOD — 1930's — DAY

Young Carl "flies" his blue balloon (the words *"The Spirit of Adventure"* hand-written on it) as he runs along the sidewalk, still wearing helmet and goggles.

*TITLE CARD: UP*

A GIRL'S VOICE drifts from a dilapidated house:

**GIRL'S VOICE (O.S.)**
*"Adventure is out there!!"*

Carl stops. He walks toward the voice.

---

### 3–4. INT. DILAPIDATED HOUSE — HALLWAY / LIVING ROOM — CONTINUOUS

Carl rounds the corner to find **ELLIE** — eight years old, mussy red hair beneath a flight helmet, bare feet, overalls dirty and patched. The old house has been transformed into a make-believe dirigible cockpit.

**YOUNG ELLIE**
*What are you doing!? Don't you know this is an exclusive club? Only explorers get in here.*

She pins her **GRAPE SODA CAP** badge onto Carl's shirt.

**YOUNG ELLIE**
*You and me, we're in a club now.*

---

### 5. INT. DILAPIDATED HOUSE — UPSTAIRS

Carl and Ellie look across the attic at the lost balloon. Between them the floor has collapsed, save one rickety beam. Carl steels his courage, starts forward, and FALLS through the floor.

---

### 9. INT. CARL'S ROOM — MOMENTS LATER

Carl in bed, arm in a CAST, reading by flashlight. Into the room floats his lost BLUE BALLOON — Ellie outside the window.

They huddle under a blanket tent. Ellie whispers:

**YOUNG ELLIE**
*I am about to let you see something I have never shown to another human being. Ever. In my life.*

She unveils: **MY ADVENTURE BOOK** — a reused photo album.

**YOUNG ELLIE**
*When I get big, I'm going where he's going: South America. "Paradise Falls, a land lost in time." I'm gonna move my clubhouse there, and park it right next to the falls.*

She flips to a page: **STUFF I'M GOING TO DO.** Past it, the pages are blank.

### MARRIED LIFE — MONTAGE (Scenes 10–37)

*A wordless sequence covering Carl and Ellie's entire life together.*

- **Wedding** — her side of the church erupts; his side applauds politely.
- They buy and renovate the old clubhouse house. Paint "Carl & Ellie" on the mailbox.
- They work at the zoo together. Their **Paradise Falls jar** slowly fills with coins — then gets broken for a new tire, a broken leg, a storm-wrecked roof.
- They age gracefully side by side, holding hands while reading, dancing by candlelight.
- Carl buys two **plane tickets to South America**. Hurries up picnic hill. Ellie falters — falls.
- **Hospital.** Ellie in the bed. She pushes the Adventure Book toward Carl. Weakly adjusts his tie. He kisses her forehead.
- Carl sits alone in the church, next to a huge bouquet of balloons.
- He walks into their house holding a single **blue balloon**.

*FADE TO BLACK.*

### 38. INT. CARL'S BEDROOM — MORNING — SEVERAL YEARS LATER

An alarm clock buzzes. **CARL** — aged, alone — shuffles out of bed, cane with four tennis balls on the bottom. His elderly assistance chair takes an eternity down the stairs.

Breakfast alone. Window cleaned. Shrine to Adventure dusted.

---

### 45–46. EXT. CARL'S NEIGHBORHOOD — CONTINUOUS

Carl's house is the **lone surviving square** not under construction. Cranes, machinery, and high-rises surround him on all sides.

**CARL** *(noticing Ellie's faded handprint on the mailbox)*
*Quite a sight, huh Ellie?*

A construction foreman offers double the last offer. Carl's leaf blower removes the foreman's hat.

**CARL** *(in megaphone)*
*You in the suit. Yes, you. Take a bath, hippy!*

---

### 48. EXT. CARL'S HOUSE — PORCH — DAY

A Wilderness Explorer stands at the door, reading from his manual:

**RUSSELL**
*"Good afternoon. My name is Russell. And I am a Wilderness Explorer in Tribe 54, Sweatlodge 12. Are you in need of any assistance today, sir?"*

Carl grudgingly gives Russell a "mission" — to find and assist a "snipe" — to get rid of him.

---

### COURTHOUSE — Carl is ordered to Shady Oaks Retirement Village.

That night, Carl makes his decision.

---

### EXT. CARL'S HOUSE — MORNING

Crowds of construction workers gather. A HISSING sound. Then —

Thousands of **BALLOONS** burst through the roof and windows. The house GROANS, shudders — and **LIFTS OFF** into the sky.

Carl looks out from his porch at the city falling away below him. He smiles.

He opens Ellie's **Adventure Book** to the map of South America.

**CARL**
*We're on our way, Ellie.*

A knock comes from the front door — Russell is clinging to the porch.
